Opening the Menu

Everything starts with a click on the HUD logo

Click the Adult HUD logo on your screen to open the main menu. A blue dialog box will appear in the centre of your screen — all features are accessed from here. The menu closes automatically after 60 seconds if nothing is selected.

What the floating text shows

The HUD shows two sets of floating text above it in-world. The lower block lists all menu options so you can see them at a glance. The upper block shows results and status messages after you use a feature. Both are only visible to you.

Hide / Show HUD Display

Toggle the floating text on and off

Sometimes you want to keep the HUD attached but hide the floating text — perhaps you're taking photos, socialising, or just want a cleaner view. This button toggles all HUD text off and back on again.

01
Open the main menu

Click the HUD logo on your screen.

02
Click the Hide button

Press the button labelled 1. 🔴 Hide. All floating text will disappear immediately.

03
To show again

Click the HUD logo again. The button will now say 1. 🟢 Show. Press it and all text returns.

Maturity Check

Find out the rating of the region you're in

Second Life regions are rated General, Moderate, or Adult. This feature checks the region you're standing in right now and tells you exactly what rating it is, what that means, and whether your viewer settings will let you access adult areas.

01
Open the menu and press 2. 🗺️

Click the HUD logo then press the 2. 🗺️ button.

02
Read the report in nearby chat

A private report appears in your Nearby Chat window — only you can see it. It shows the region name, its maturity rating, the parcel name, and personalised advice about your current viewer settings.

03
Check the HUD display prim

A quick colour-coded summary also appears on the HUD's floating display — 🟢 General, 🟡 Moderate, or 🔴 Adult — so you can see it at a glance without opening chat.

What the ratings mean
  • 🟢 General (PG) — family-friendly, no adult content allowed
  • 🟡 Moderate (M) — clubs, mild themes, some suggestive content
  • 🔴 Adult (A) — explicit content permitted, age verification required
💡
Can't reach adult areas?

Run a Maturity Check first. The report will tell you exactly which viewer setting to change and where to find it. In Firestorm: Avatar › Preferences › General › tick Adult. In the official viewer: Me › Preferences › General › select Adult.

Safe Word

Emergency stop for any scene — one tap

A safe word is a word or phrase that immediately signals you want everything to stop. When you fire the Safe Word feature, the HUD shouts your chosen word on public chat and sends an RLV release command — removing any scripted restrictions that may be active on your avatar.

01
Open the menu and press 3. 🆘

Click the HUD logo then press the 3. 🆘 button. A confirmation dialog will appear — this prevents accidental firing.

02
Confirm by pressing 🆘 FIRE

Press the 🆘 FIRE button. Your safe word will be shouted publicly, and the RLV @clear command is sent to release any active restrictions. The HUD display flashes a warning so you know it worked.

03
To change your safe word

In the same confirmation dialog, press ✏️ Change Word. A text box will appear. Type your new word or phrase and press Submit. It is saved immediately and shown in the HUD header.

Default safe word

The HUD comes pre-set with RED as the default safe word. This is one of the most widely recognised safe words in SL communities. You can change it to anything you and your partner have agreed on — just keep it short and memorable.

⚠️
Important

RLV must be enabled in your viewer for the @clear command to work. If you haven't enabled RLV, the safe word shout still works — your partner will hear it — but scripted restrictions may not release automatically. See the Resource Guide for RLV setup instructions.

Inspect Avatar

Learn about someone nearby before you approach

This feature scans for avatars within 96 metres of you, lets you pick one from a list, and shows you a profile report — their display name, account age, what group tag they're showing, and the names of any non-HUD attachments visible on their avatar.

01
Open the menu and press 4. 🔍

Click the HUD logo then press 4. 🔍. The HUD display will show Scanning… while it searches. This takes just a moment.

02
Pick an avatar from the list

A dialog appears listing nearby avatars with their names and distance in metres. Press the button for the person you want to inspect. You can see up to 9 avatars per scan.

03
Read the report in nearby chat

A private report appears in your Nearby Chat. While the report is loading, the HUD looks up their account creation date — this may take a second or two, then the full report appears automatically.

What the report includes
  • Display name and avatar key (unique ID)
  • Distance from you in metres
  • Account creation date (born date)
  • Names of visible non-HUD attachments
💡
Why can't I see their HUD or all their attachments?

SL only allows scripts to read the names of non-HUD attachments worn by nearby avatars. HUD items and some private attachments are deliberately hidden by the platform for privacy. This is a system limitation, not a bug.

Teleport Board

Jump to curated adult destinations and your saved spots

The Teleport Board is your quick-access list of adult destinations. It contains a set of curated hub locations built into the HUD, plus any locations you have saved yourself. Selecting one opens the SL world map ready to teleport — you click Teleport to confirm.

01
Open the menu and press 5. 🚪

Click the HUD logo then press 5. 🚪. The teleport board dialog opens, listing all available destinations.

02
Choose a destination

Press the button for where you want to go. Curated locations show as plain names. Your own saved locations appear with a 📍 icon in front.

03
Confirm the teleport on the world map

The SL world map opens automatically, centred on your chosen destination. Click the Teleport button on the map to travel there.

Built-in curated destinations
  • Adult Hub — the main welcome and onboarding hub
  • Zindra Hub — Linden Lab's official adult continent
  • Kink Hub — gateway for BDSM and kink communities
  • The Playground — social hub with events and shopping
💡
Your saved locations appear here too

Any place you save using the 📍 Save Location feature (slot 6) automatically appears in the Teleport Board list. So once you've found a favourite spot, you can jump back to it directly from the board.

Save Location

Bookmark up to 5 of your favourite adult spots

Found a great venue, club or region you want to return to? Save Location stores your current position — region name and exact coordinates — directly inside the HUD object using permanent storage. Your saved spots survive script resets, relogs, and even if you give the HUD to someone else.

01
Stand where you want to save

Walk or teleport to the exact spot you want to bookmark. The HUD saves your coordinates at the moment you press the button, so position yourself first.

02
Open the menu and press 6. 📍

The Save Location dialog opens. It shows where you are right now, how many slots are used, and the names of your existing saved locations.

03
Press 💾 Save Here

Press the 💾 Save Here button. The location is saved immediately, named after the region you're in. A confirmation message appears in nearby chat and on the HUD display.

To delete a saved location
01
Open Save Location and press 🗑️ Delete One

From the Save Location dialog press 🗑️ Delete One. A new dialog lists all your saved spots.

02
Press the ✖ button next to the location to remove

Press the ✖ button for the location you want to remove. It is deleted immediately and the remaining slots shift up automatically.

💡
Maximum 5 locations

The HUD stores up to 5 locations. When all slots are full the Save Here button changes to 🚫 Full (5/5). Delete an existing location first to make room for a new one.

Adjust Display

Move and recolour the floating text

The floating text that appears above the HUD in-world can be nudged up or down if it overlaps other objects or UI elements. You can also change its colour to suit your preference.

01
Open the menu and press 9. 📐

Click the HUD logo then press 9. 📐. The Adjust Display dialog opens, showing the current offset and colour.

02
Move the display prim

Press ▲ Up or ▼ Down to nudge the floating text 1cm at a time. The dialog stays open so you can press multiple times. The position is saved permanently to the object — it persists after relog.

03
Change the text colour

Press Default, Green, or White to change the colour of the HUD's idle text. Result output colours (yellow for status, green for lists) are not affected.

04
Restore original position

If you've moved the display too far, press ↩ Restore to return it to where it was when you first attached the HUD this session.

💡
Position range

The display prim can move up to 13cm up or 13cm down from its original position. The dialog always shows your current offset so you know how far you've gone.
