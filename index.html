FAQs & Answers
10 entries
My avatar looks like a cloud / grey blob — how do I fix it? Appearance +

This is a bake fail — your avatar textures haven't loaded properly.

1
Wait 30 seconds — resolves itself on slow connections.
2
Avatar menu → Avatar Health → Refresh Attachments
3
Avatar → Avatar Health → Rebake Textures (shortcut: Ctrl+Alt+R)
4
Still grey? Change to a different region, or relog entirely.
// Mentor Tip

If stuck as "Ruth" (old default), have them wear a complete outfit from Library: Inventory → Library → Avatars.

My attachment / HUD won't attach or keeps disappearing. Appearance +

Usually caused by inventory not fully loading or high sim lag.

1
Check Inventory → Recent — wait for the spinning wheel to stop.
2
Right-click the item → Attach To → choose attach point manually.
3
If vanished: Edit → Detach Object — it may have attached off-screen.
4
Relog on a quieter, lower-traffic region.
// Mentor Tip

If a HUD seems lost, try Avatar → Avatar Health → Reset Skeleton & Animations and relog.

I can't find items in my inventory — items seem missing. Inventory +

Items are rarely truly lost. Work through these steps:

1
Use the inventory search bar — try partial names or creator names.
2
Check the Recent and Received Items tabs.
3
Check the Trash folder — items can end up there accidentally.
4
Recently purchased? Check Recent Items or the Objects subfolder.
5
Firestorm: Avatar → Avatar Health → Clear Inventory Cache, then relog.
// Mentor Tip

Marketplace purchases can be re-delivered at no charge from the resident's order history at marketplace.secondlife.com.

Teleport failed / I'm stuck and can't move. Movement +

Teleport failures are common under high server load.

1
Wait 60 seconds — ghost avatars need time to catch up.
2
Teleport home: World → Teleport Home.
3
If stuck in place: sit on a nearby object, then stand.
4
Last resort: secondlife.com → My Account → Return Home.
// Mentor Tip

Remind newcomers about flying: Page Up to ascend, Page Down to descend, F to toggle fly mode (viewer-dependent).

How do I get Linden Dollars (L$)? Economy +
1
Buy L$: Me → Buy L$ in viewer, or via LindeX on secondlife.com. Rate: approx 250–270 L$ per US$1.
2
Earn in-world: Work as host, DJ, model, scripter, or builder — many roles pay L$/hour.
3
Premium stipend: Premium members receive a weekly L$ allowance automatically.
// Mentor Tip

Failed purchase? Direct residents to Help → Support Portal → Billing. Never ask for or accept L$ as a mentor.

How do groups work? I'm getting too many notifications. Groups +

Limits: Free accounts can join up to 42 groups; Premium up to 70.

Joining: Search via People → Groups → Search or click a group sign in-world.

Silencing notices: Right-click the group → Edit → uncheck Receive Notices.

Active tag: Only one group tag shows at a time — click the group → press Activate.

// Mentor Tip

Newcomers often accidentally leave important groups. Remind them to check People → Groups regularly.

How do I get my own land / place to live? Land +

Linden Homes (Premium): Premium members get a free Linden Home — themes include Bellisseria, Traditional, Houseboats, Stilt Houses. Claim via secondlife.com → My Account → Get a Linden Home.

Renting from estates: Many estate owners rent parcels for L$/week. Search Land Rental on Marketplace or in-world classifieds.

Buying mainland: Requires Premium and uses the L$ land auction system. Not recommended for newcomers.

// Mentor Tip

Newcomers often confuse "renting" (paying L$ to a private landlord) with "owning" (paying tier to Linden Lab). Always clarify the difference.

What's the difference between Basic and Premium membership? Account +
🔓
Basic (Free): Full world access, 42 groups, Marketplace, L$ buying. Cannot own land or claim a Linden Home.
⭐
Premium: ~300 L$/week stipend, free Linden Home, 70 groups, Premium sandboxes, 24/7 live chat support.
💎
Premium Plus: Higher stipend, more groups, additional perks — designed for power users.
// Mentor Tip

Encourage newcomers to explore the free tier fully before upgrading — most features are available at no cost.

Someone is harassing me / griefing. What do I do? Rules +
1
Mute/Block immediately: right-click avatar → Block.
2
Move away — teleport to a different area or home.
3
File an Abuse Report: Help → Report Abuse, choose category, describe what happened, submit.
4
Document — take screenshots with Ctrl+Shift+S.

As a mentor, do not intervene directly with griefers — report only.

// Mentor Tip

Blocking is local to the viewer only — it doesn't globally ban the user. Only Linden Lab can do that via the AR process.

Voice chat isn't working — I can't hear anyone. Audio +
1
Me → Preferences → Sound & Media — confirm Voice enabled and correct devices selected.
2
Confirm the region/parcel allows voice — check the voice dot indicator near the minimap.
3
Check system audio — ensure SL isn't muted at the OS level.
4
Click the microphone icon in the toolbar to toggle voice on/off.
5
Relog — voice sometimes fails on the initial login.
// Mentor Tip

Firestorm users: toggling Preferences → Sound & Media → Enable Voice off and on resolves most intermittent drop-outs.

How can I have a good avatar for free? Appearance +

You can put together a solid, modern-looking avatar without spending a single L$. Here's how:

1
Your starter body is already a Legacy body — this is a quality mesh body, so you're ahead of the game from day one.
2
Adjust your shape: Right-click your avatar → Edit Shape. Tweak sliders for height, build, face — make it yours.
3
Get the Mike Head from the Firestorm Welcome Centre — it's free, high quality, and pairs well with the Legacy body.
4
Find free clothes — visit The Free Dove (search in World) or browse the Marketplace filtered by price: L$0.
5
Check Inventory → Library → Clothing for starter outfits that came with your account.
// Mentor Tip

The Firestorm Welcome Centre is a goldmine for freebies — not just the Mike Head. Walk residents through it; there are skins, shapes, and outfits all at no cost. Make sure clothing is labelled as compatible with the Legacy body before they wear it.

Mentor Tips & Scripts
6 scripts
Greeting a New Resident Opening +

Keep your opener friendly, brief, and non-intrusive. Many newcomers feel overwhelmed — don't flood them with information right away. Give them space to respond.

// Say in local or IM
Hi there! Welcome to Second Life! 😊 I'm a Mentor volunteer — feel free to ask me anything, no question is too basic. Happy to help you get started!

If they don't respond, give it 2–3 minutes before a gentle follow-up. Some residents are still figuring out how to type in chat.

Resident Seems Confused or Overwhelmed Support +

Slow down. Ask one question at a time. Mirror their pace — if they're typing slowly, don't flood them with walls of text. Identify one goal and focus on it entirely.

// Say in local or IM
No worries at all — SL can feel overwhelming at first! Let's take it step by step. What's the one thing you'd most like to do right now?

If they can't articulate a goal, offer concrete options: explore somewhere new, customise their avatar, find people to meet.

When You Don't Know the Answer Best Practice +

It's always better to say you don't know than to give incorrect information. Misinformation causes real frustration — residents trust mentors.

// Say in local or IM
That's a great question — I want to give you the right answer rather than guess. Let me find out / you can also check the SL Knowledge Base at community.secondlife.com.
community.secondlife.com — official knowledge base and Q&A
Second Life Answers — community forum for resident questions
Firestorm Support Group (in-world) — viewer-specific issues
Handling Rude or Difficult Residents Boundaries +

Mentors are volunteers. You are never obligated to tolerate abuse. Stay calm, remain professional, and disengage if needed — your wellbeing comes first.

// Say in local or IM
I'm happy to help, but I do need us to keep the conversation respectful. If you'd like assistance, I'm here — otherwise I'll let you explore on your own.

If they continue, simply stop responding. Mute them and report to a senior mentor or via AR if they are harassing others. Do not engage in arguments.

Wrapping Up a Conversation Closing +

End every interaction positively — even brief ones. Leave the door open for follow-up questions. A summary of what you covered helps them remember the steps.

// Say in local or IM
Great talking with you! If you have any more questions later, just shout — mentors are usually around. Enjoy Second Life! 🌟

Log any recurring confusion patterns you notice — valuable feedback for the wider mentor team.

Explaining Mesh Bodies to Newcomers Appearance +

Mesh bodies are one of the most confusing topics for newcomers. Use a simple analogy before going into technical detail.

// Analogy to use
Think of the default avatar as a basic mannequin. A mesh body is like a custom suit you wear over it — much better detail and fits modern clothing. Most popular are Maitreya (female) and Legacy or Signature (male). They cost L$ but free demos let you try before you buy!
Mesh bodies need BOM (Bakes on Mesh) enabled for skin to display correctly.
Clothing must be fitted/rigged for that specific body — not all clothes fit all bodies.
Always demo first — demos are free and show exactly how it looks on your shape.
Free alternative: search Ruth 2.0 on the Marketplace.

The CLARA Method
De-escalation framework

CLARA is a five-step framework for nonviolent communication and de-escalation. It shifts a conversation from a win-lose argument to a collaborative dialogue — ideal for mentors handling frustrated or confrontational residents.

C
Centre Yourself
Before engaging with someone angry or confrontational, manage your own internal state first.
// Action
Take a breath, ground yourself, and remind yourself that the other person's anger is likely about their own needs or fears — not a personal attack. If you aren't calm, you cannot calm someone else.
L
Listen
Give the person your full, undivided attention without interrupting.
// Action
Look for the feeling or need behind their words. Even if they are being rude or irrational — what are they trying to protect? What do they feel they have lost?
A
Affirm (or Acknowledge)
Find a point of connection or a feeling you can validate — to lower their defensive wall.
// Action
This doesn't mean you agree with their logic. You are simply affirming their experience.
"I can hear how frustrated you are that the rules seem to have changed without warning."
"It makes sense that you feel ignored right now."
R
Respond
Only after affirming their feelings do you address the actual issue or provide information.
// Action
Use "I" statements and keep your tone neutral. Avoid blame. Address the specific points they raised during the Listen phase.
A
Add (or Ask / Adjust)
Wrap up by providing additional resources, asking a clarifying question, or adjusting the situation.
// Action
Offer a next step that moves toward resolution or a graceful exit.
"Would it help if we looked at the guidelines together?"
"How can we make this right for both of us?"
✔ Pros
Defuses Hostility
By affirming the other person, you stop the back-and-forth of an argument before it escalates.
Preserves Relationships
Builds a reputation for fairness and level-headedness — especially valuable in mentoring or leadership roles.
Clarity
Helps you get to the actual problem rather than just fighting about the surface anger.
✘ Cons
Time-Consuming
Requires patience and can take longer than simply giving an order or walking away.
Emotional Tax
Requires you to absorb some of the other person's heat while staying cool yourself.
⚠ Not for Physical Danger
If a situation turns violent or physically unsafe, CLARA is not a substitute for removing yourself or seeking safety.
