Age & Maturity

Getting Access

A resident needs two things before they can see adult SL: age verification on their Linden Lab account, and the correct maturity rating enabled in their viewer. These are separate — verifying the account alone won't show adult regions until the viewer filter is changed.

Age Requirement

All SL residents must be 18 or older. Age is asserted at account creation. Direct residents to accounts.secondlife.com if they need to check or update their details.

📋 Copy-paste — Age verification Copy You need to be 18 or older to use Second Life at all. Adult content and the Adult maturity rating are only available to verified accounts — you can check your account details at accounts.secondlife.com. If you're already signed up you're fine; the next step is enabling the right maturity rating in your viewer.

📋 Copy-paste — Enabling Adult content Copy In Firestorm, go to Avatar › Preferences › General and tick General, Moderate and Adult under Maturity Rating. On the official Second Life viewer it's Me › Preferences › General, then set ‘Maturity ratings I want to see’ to Adult. Both take effect straight away — you may need to teleport or relog to see new regions listed in Search.

Explicit sexual content and mature themes. Only visible with Adult rating enabled.

Portals & Destinations

Where to Send Them

Adult portal hubs are rated-A gateway regions packed with teleport boards, freebies and community info. They're a safer first stop than throwing a resident into a random adult venue. As always — send the resident to the destination search in their viewer (Ctrl+F) rather than giving out SLURLs directly.

📋 Copy-paste — Where to go first Copy Press Ctrl+F to open Search, switch to Destinations, make sure Adult is ticked, and search ‘adult hub’. The Adult Hub is a good first stop — it's a welcome area with info boards, freebies and teleport boards to other adult regions. Zindra is the official adult continent if you want somewhere to explore, and the Playground is more of a social/shopping hub.
What Mentors should expect

Most adult regions have an age-gate door on arrival — click and confirm 18+. Some require joining a group for full access. Explicit content will be visible — that's normal for Adult regions. Posted rules vary by venue; if a resident asks about a specific region, ask them to read the rules card at the landing point first.

Tip for new residents: Before leaving a welcome hub, advise them to create a Landmark (World › Landmark This Place). It's the easiest way back if they get lost.
Mentor Note — No Adult SLURLs

Never paste adult-region SLURLs in public channels or in the Newcomer page. Use search terms and let the resident find it via Destinations. Campwich Woodland Cabin is Mentors Only — it should never appear in public materials under any circumstances.

Bodies & Genitalia

Building A Body

Default SL avatars don't have functional genitalia. When a resident asks how to ‘do sex’ in SL, the usual answer is: upgrade to a mesh body first, then add a compatible genital add-on. Most residents upgrade their body anyway for clothing choice, so the adult path piggybacks on that.

♂️
Male Default Body

Completely flat and smooth at the groin. No genital mesh of any kind. Upgrade required for any adult-capable anatomy.

♀️
Female Default Body

A very basic, non-detailed groin shape is present but it is not interactive or realistic. Still requires an add-on for adult use.

Sex Furniture

How It Works

‘Sex furniture’ is any in-world object containing animations for adult poses — beds, sofas, bondage equipment, wall mounts, portable pose balls. The object drives the animation; the resident just sits and picks from a menu.

01
Sit on it

Right-click the furniture and select Sit, or simply touch it. Some objects use a menu ball nearby.

02
Choose a pose

A dialog menu lists available poses or activities. Select one — the avatar moves into position automatically.

03
Invite others (optional)

Multi-avatar furniture will prompt nearby people to join. They must accept — nobody is force-sat.

04
Adjust position

Most furniture has X/Y/Z nudge buttons in the menu so residents can align their specific body shape. Some save settings.

05
Stand up to exit

Press Page Up or click Stand. Residents are always free to leave any animation at any time.

📋 Copy-paste — Using sex furniture Copy To use sex furniture, right-click the object and pick Sit (or touch it if there's a menu ball nearby). A dialog menu will appear with poses or activities — pick one and your avatar moves into place automatically. For multi-avatar furniture, the other person has to accept the invite — nobody can be force-sat. You can nudge position with the X/Y/Z buttons in the menu, and Page Up or Stand lets you out at any time.

XTAC / XTC

Widely used, clean menus, good sync. The most common engine a resident will encounter.

RLV / RLVA

Advanced engine enabling automatic body interaction and restraint play. Requires RLV enabled in the viewer.

Newer furniture that animates finger, toe and facial Bento bones for expressive, natural movement.

Interaction specific to Xcite body parts. Adds touch, arousal and response systems.

Buying Checklist

When a resident asks what to check before buying: Bento support · pose count (20+ is good) · body compatibility list · land impact (LI) · copy/modify permissions · demo room available.

Consent & Safety

Consent Matters

This is the most common area where a newcomer gets themselves into trouble — not by malice, but by assuming adult spaces mean open permission. Consent is required for everything. If you're fielding a resident complaint, nine times out of ten the root cause is that one side didn't ask first.

🛡️
Built-in SL Protections

A resident must click to sit on anything. Objects must request permission to animate the avatar. Scripts must request permission to attach items. They can always stand up.

🔒
RLV — Their Choice

Restrained Love Viewer is off by default. Enable only with trusted partners. In Firestorm: Avatar › Preferences › Firestorm › RLV.

🔴
Safe Words

Agree a safe word before any restricted-play scene. ‘Red’ or ‘Safeword’ are universally understood. A good partner will offer one up front.

🚫
Block & Report

Right-click any avatar › Block to stop all contact. Help › Report Abuse for ToS violations. Report rule-breaking to region moderators.

📋 Copy-paste — Consent basics Copy Consent in SL means a clear yes before any sexual interaction — sitting someone on furniture, touching them, explicit messages, or starting roleplay. Silence isn't yes; enthusiasm is. If unsure, ask. If someone says no, accept it and move on — pressing after a no is harassment and can be reported.
What Consent Means Here

Clear yes before any sexual interaction — sitting someone on furniture, touching them, sending explicit messages, or starting roleplay. Silence is not a yes. Enthusiasm is. If unsure, ask. Most people in adult SL are happy to talk openly about what they do and don't want. This is considered normal and respectful, not awkward.

No Means No — Always

If someone declines — for any reason, or no reason at all — the resident should accept it gracefully and move on. No pressure, no bargaining, no guilt, no repeating. This applies to everyone regardless of gender, avatar appearance, or prior behaviour. Persistence after a no is harassment and can result in a region ban or an abuse report to Linden Lab.

📋 Copy-paste — Block & Report Copy If someone is bothering you, right-click their avatar and choose Block — that cuts off all IMs, chat and interaction from them. For anything that breaks the Terms of Service (harassment, age issues, non-consensual content) use Help › Report Abuse — Linden Lab reviews every report. For rule-breaking at a specific venue, also contact the region's moderators or group owners. You're always free to log off or teleport away — no explanation needed.
Personal Safety reminder: Residents should never share real-world personal information in SL — no real name, location, phone number, or photos. Advise caution with anyone who rushes intimacy or applies pressure. They're always free to log off — no explanation needed.
The Golden Rule

Consent is not just a rule — it's the culture that makes adult SL work. Communities that thrive are ones where members look after each other. Passing that on is the most useful thing a Mentor does here.

Mentor Notes

For Mentors

A few things that are Mentor-facing only — not for paste to residents.

Your Comfort Comes First

Not every Mentor wants to field adult-content questions — and that's completely fine. If you'd rather not engage, say so politely and hand off. There's never any expectation to discuss adult topics in any mentoring role, and no resident is entitled to your time on this subject. ‘Sorry, that's outside what I cover — try Destinations search for «adult hub»’ is a complete answer.

When to hand off

Hand off a question rather than guessing if: you're not sure about a specific product or body compatibility; the resident is describing distress, harassment or non-consensual behaviour (point them at Report Abuse, then let another Mentor or Linden Lab take it from there); the conversation is drifting toward 1:1 roleplay or personal attention that isn't mentoring.

Keep it community-standard

This page is framed as Mentor reference, not an LL-endorsed resource. Creator and product names are listed for recognition only — no endorsements, no direct Marketplace links out. If in doubt about a specific piece of content, raise it with Viola before adding it.
