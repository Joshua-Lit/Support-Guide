What is Bellisseria?
Continent overview · the basics in plain language
▼

Bellisseria is a Linden-Lab-owned continent that hosts the second generation of Linden Homes — the homes included with paid memberships. It launched in 2019, has grown to over 2,800 regions, and is built and maintained by the Moles (a small team of long-time SL residents employed by Linden Lab).

Unlike older themed estates, Bellisseria blends themes across one connected continent: a Victorian neighbourhood might sit beside a houseboat dock, with a log-home valley a short railway ride away. The whole thing is navigable by boat, train, road, and air.

Who lives there
Plus, Premium, and Premium Plus members. Basic accounts cannot claim a Linden Home but are welcome to visit.
Parcel sizes
512 m² (Plus), 1,024 m² (Premium), and 2,048 m² (Premium Plus exclusive themes).
Use restrictions
Residential only — no commerce, rentals, or advertising (except Ridgewood Enclave's commercial districts). No ban lines are allowed; security orbs only. Skyboxes permitted above 2,000m.
Staying in theme
Parcels must be kept presentable and broadly in keeping with their neighbourhood's theme.

Linden Home themes
All current themes · tier eligibility · quick descriptions
▼
Tier guide: Plus = 512m² · Premium = 1,024m² · Premium Plus = 2,048m² (exclusive themes). Most themes also offer an open-plan variant of each model with fewer interior walls. To see every theme and model in person, send residents to BelliHub — the official walkthrough region.
Traditional Premium
Suburban one and two-storey homes with shingle roofs, gardens, and picket fences. The continent's heart and most-modded theme — Winchester in particular has a huge add-on community.
Houseboats Premium
Floating homes moored along waterways with private boat-rezzing docks. Newer styles (Evening Star, Jolly Roger) include a colour HUD for wall tints; older styles don't.
Campers Plus
Compact 512 m² parcels with a vintage caravan. Wooded camping-ground feel. Entry-level option for Plus members.
Victorian Premium
Gaslit cobbles, ornate trim, parks with reflecting pools. 32×32m parcels with eight models featuring turrets, bay windows, expansive porches — including Shelley, Hardy and Verne (two-floor) and Doyle (single-floor). Connected by an extensive railway.
Log Homes Premium
Upscale log cabins in forested hills and valleys. Eight styles with high ceilings and two floors — Timberline is the only one with a fireplace. Hiking and horse-riding country.
Stilt Homes Premium
Coastal homes raised on stilts, inspired by the southeastern US. Four styles — including Havana (3-room cross-shape) and Tortuga (open-plan single-storey). Some on the beach, some over water. House controller sits in the water.
Chalets Premium
Alpine cabins among mountains and pine, with their own railway. Four base styles (Matterhorn, Eiger, Mont Blanc, Jungfrau) each in a closed or open-plan variant — eight choices in total.
Fantasy Premium
Whimsical dwellings — treehouses, mushroom homes, magical themes. Six base models (Amberbrooke, Fairybrooke, Mistbrooke, Rosebrooke, Stonebrooke and one more) each with an open-plan variant. Includes the Fairstone Quarry wedding venue.
Newbrooke Plus Premium
Modern suburban homes with customisable siding, trim and brickwork colours. Eight models — Aldridge, Bradbury, Coniston, Heaton (1024m² only) and Denver, Ender, Faraday, Gatewood (either 512 or 1024m²).
Sakura Plus Premium
Japanese-inspired homes among cherry blossoms. Eight models named after Japanese flowers — Shion, Ajisai, Renge, Kaneshon (1024m²) and Kosumosu, Tenjikubotan, Botan, Himawari (either size). Each available with closed or open-plan layout.
Ranch Premium Plus
Large 2,048 m² parcels with ranch-style houses, paddocks, and rural landscapes. Horse country.
Mediterranean Premium Plus
Coastal villas in pastel rose and ochre, with terracotta tiles, stucco and adobe. 2,048m² parcels with four base styles, each up to eleven rooms or open-plan. Has its own railway.
Thousand Island Premium Plus
Lakeside homes set across an archipelago of small islands. Quiet, water-focused theme for Premium Plus residents.
Aspen Ridge Premium
Mountain homes with seasonal landscape changes — the regions visibly shift through the seasons.
Ridgewood Enclave Premium Plus
Modern Premium Plus homes among palm trees and tropical landscaping. Six unique designs with optional layout variations. Now also hosts Bellisseria's first commercial districts (March 2026) — the only place commerce is allowed on the continent.
Copperfield Heights All tiers
2026 release. Craftsman-style Arts & Crafts homes with warm wood tones, exposed beams and natural stone. 12 standard floor plans + 8 open variants. First Bellisseria theme to mix Plus, Premium, and Premium Plus parcels in one community.
First-generation themes (Tahoe, Elderglen, Sahreta Osumai, Meadowbrook) still exist on older estates but are no longer claimable. Some long-term residents still occupy them. They use older covenants and have a 175-prim limit on 512 m².

How to claim a Linden Home
Step-by-step · what to tell a newcomer asking
▼
Have an eligible membership Plus, Premium, or Premium Plus. Basic accounts cannot claim a Linden Home. Tier upgrade is done at secondlife.com → My Account → Membership.
Check land allowance A Linden Home counts against the resident's land allowance. If they already own mainland that uses their full tier, they need to free up some allowance first.
Go to the claim page secondlife.com → My Account → Get a Linden Home. Available themes are listed by tier, with home models, parcel sizes, and a region map.
Browse and pick a parcel Filter by theme. Click an available parcel to preview its surroundings on the map. Don't rush — neighbours and views matter and swapping is easy if it doesn't work out.
Confirm and teleport Once claimed, the resident gets a teleport landmark and a House Controller appears just outside the parcel boundary (or in the water, for stilt homes).
Furnishing & customising The House Controller (touch it on the parcel) controls textures, accessories, and reset. Furniture is up to the resident — Bellisseria covenants forbid replacing the house structure itself.
Premium Plus tip: Premium Plus members can request a specific Linden Home via a support ticket if a particular parcel becomes available. They also get exclusive access to Ranch, Mediterranean, Thousand Island, and Ridgewood Enclave themes.

Abandoning & swapping: About Land → General → Abandon Land releases a Linden Home back to the pool. Residents can claim a different one immediately — but Linden Lab limits this to 5 swaps per 24 hours to keep the pool fair for everyone.

The main resident-run group for everyone living on or visiting the continent. Daily chatter, event announcements, mutual help. Searchable in Groups.
Bellisseria Bureau
Resident-run news outlet covering Bellisseria life, theme reveals, community events, and Mole news. Worth keeping an eye on for current happenings.

Getting around
Trains, boats, roads, balloons · how the continent connects
▼

One of Bellisseria's defining features is that it's fully traversable. Residents can travel from any region to any other without teleporting — by rail, water, road, or air. Several rezzing zones are scattered across the continent for vehicles.

🚂
Railway network
Continent-spanning rail system with separate networks for Victorian, Chalet, and Mediterranean regions. Trains rez at marked stations — touch a station sign to ride.
⛵
Waterways
Bellisseria is built around navigable canals, rivers, and ocean. Boat rezzers at marina parcels and community docks. Houseboats and stilt homes have private rezzers.
🚗
Roads
A road network connects neighbourhoods. Vehicle rezzing zones are placed at community hubs — touch the rez board or use a rezzer parcel.
🎈
Air & balloons
Hot-air balloon rezzers exist at several spots for slow scenic tours. Aircraft are permitted but be mindful of altitude limits and neighbouring parcels.
🚲
Bicycles & horses
Free bicycle rezzers at many community areas. Some Linden Home residents keep horses (or dragons, or moths) and ride them between parcels.
📍
Rezzing zones
Marked public rezzing zones let any resident temporarily rez a vehicle on otherwise-restricted parcels. Look for the signposts at major hubs.
Note for newcomers: Vehicles can be tricky for new residents — sim crossings still occasionally cause hiccups. If a resident is struggling, suggest a teleport for now and an exploration trip once they're more comfortable with the viewer.

Useful resources
Where to send a resident wanting more · reference blogs · community sites
▼

Linden Lab does not publish floor plans for the homes — the official answer is to send residents to the BelliHub demo region or Sandbox Bellisseria to walk through every model in person. The community resources below fill in much of the rest.

Inara Pey's blog
The closest thing to authoritative third-party documentation. Detailed release coverage of every theme with model-by-model descriptions. modemworld.me/tag/linden-homes
Bellisseria Citizens site
Resident-curated destinations, community spots, and continent landmarks — companion to the in-world group. sites.google.com/view/bellisseriacitizens
Add-ons list (community)
A community-maintained inventory of marketplace add-ons organised by home model name — useful when a resident asks for a porch, pergola or expansion kit for their specific home. bellisseriacommunity.wixsite.com
Second Glances with Arabella
Reflective theme-by-theme reviews of Linden Homes with screenshots and resident perspective. arabellawindsor.com
Official SL wiki
Linden Lab's official theme documentation including model names, parcel sizes, and release notes. wiki.secondlife.com
Linden Lab knowledge base
Official help docs covering covenants, house controller features, abandon/swap procedures, and support ticket routes. community.secondlife.com
